How It Works

4

Easy Steps

We’ve all been there. You have an opportunity for a great new project. You’ve got the talent and skills to deliver, but you don't know how much you should charge. In fact, you’re not really sure where to start. Pricetag can help. We take the guessing out of quoting and give you peace of mind. Give it a try.

Step 1:

Sign Up

Ok, so you've picked the plan that's right for you. Now it's time to create a profile. We just need the standard stuff: your name, company name, email, password and payment information. That's it! Short and sweet.

Step 2:

Tell us about your company

Here’s where you let us know a little bit more about yourself. Upload your company logo. Key in your team members. You can do it by name, title, or a mix of both. Now type in their rates. Either by hour or annual salary is fine, because Pricetag does all the necessary conversions for you. What are your overhead costs? Any standard expenses? Desired profit percentage? All of these things go into creating your company profile. And you only have to input this information once. Once it’s in there, it’s applied to every quote. Got to love efficiency!

Step 3:

Create your first quote

Now for the nitty gritty. Tell us the name of the project and what’s required to do it. Look and feel, rounds of designs, front and back end development, maybe? Select the appropriate tasks from our list--or key in your own unique ones--and assign your team members to them. And what about the project itself? Does it look easy or tough? Our Risk Bar allows you to build in the appropriate cushion. Now, are there any additional costs associated with the project? Travel expenses or server charges, for example? Add those in. And what about project assumptions? Plug those in too. Pricetag gently guides you through these questions to help ensure all your bases are covered. And it also offers you the flexibility to make sure your quote covers everything necessary for your unique project.

Step 4:

View and send your first quote

And voila! Your quote is done. Sporting your company logo at the top, and clean and clear formatting, it’s looking sharp. You can print it, email it, or export it as a PDF. Or you can go back and tinker with it some more. Now go win that job!
